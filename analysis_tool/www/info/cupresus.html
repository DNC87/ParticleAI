Taxa included: all species of the Cupressaceae family, the main species are cemetery cypress (Cupressus sempervirens), arizona cypress (Cupressus arizonica), Monterey cypress (Cupressus macrocarpa), common tuya (Platycladus orientalis), California white cedar (Calocedrus decurrens).
Description: monoes trees or shrubs; unisexual strobiles, with opposite scales.
Phenology: winter flowering or early spring.
Ecology: cultivated as ornamentals, except for junipers.
Pollen morphology: generally unreacted grains, microgranulated heterogeneously (orbited), 15-30 microns.

Allergenicity: High.
